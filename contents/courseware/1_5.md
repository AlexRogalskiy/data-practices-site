<section>
	<div class="full">
		<h2>Refining Your Hypothesis (from 1.4)</h2>
		<ul>
			<li>Your questions, and profiling should give you a good feel for the data</li>
			<li>Now it’s time to create a hypothesis to test (if you haven’t already)</li>
			<li>Don’t be afraid to make bold claims, your hypothesis can (and almost certainly will) change</li>
			<li>As you explore the data, be open to it creating new hypotheses</li>
			<li>Feel free to test using informal (ex: data visualization) or formal (statistical tests/models) techniques to (in)validate and evolve quickly</li>
			<li>Once you cycle through some exploration it should naturally transition to full-blown analysis</li>
		</ul>

	<div class="full">
		<h2>Hindsight VS Insight VS Foresight</h2>
	</div>
	<div class="one-third">
		<h4>Hindsight</h4>
		<p>[IMAGE]</p>
		<p>The journey of data analysis often needs to start with <b>hindsight</b> - taking stock of data from past events and searching for an explanatory hypothesis.</p>
	</div>
	<div class="one-third">
		<h4>Insight</h4>
		<p>[IMAGE]</p>
		<p>In analyzing that data, you will uncover <b>insights</b> as to what patterns exist in the data, and what variables contribute to outcomes.</p>
	</div>
	<div class="one-third">
		<h4>Foresight</h4>
		<p>[IMAGE]</p>
		<p>From those insights, you can develop <b>foresight</b> - the ability to predict how certain actions or external factors will impact future outcomes.</p>
	</div>

<section>
	<div class="full">
		<h2>Types of Analysis</h2>
		<p>Related to the notion of Hindsight, Insight, and Foresight, you can classify types of analysis in four main categories:</p>
	</div>
	<div class="one-fourth">
		<h4>Descriptive</h4>
		<p>[IMAGE]</p>
		<p>Analyze what is happening now and in the past by characterizing data and uncovering patterns</p>
	</div>
	<div class="one-fourth">
		<h4>Diagnostic</h4>
		<p>[IMAGE]</p>
		<p>Determine what happened and why, determine the causal and correlative relationships between variables and outcomes</p>
	</div>
	<div class="one-fourth">
		<h4>Predictive</h4>
		<p>[IMAGE]</p>
		<p>Forecast future events based on extrapolating from past data</p>
	</div>
	<div class="one-fourth">
		<h4>Prescriptive</h4>
		<p>[IMAGE]</p>
		<p>Determine next steps, recommend actions to take to achieve a specific outcome</p>
	</div>
	<aside class="notes">
	Notes
	</aside>
</section>

<section>
	<div class="full">
		<h2>Governance</h2>
	</div>
	<div class="half text-align-left">
		<p>Data Governance is the practice of ensuring the usability, quality, security, and availability of data within an organization.</p>
		<ul>
			<li><b>Data Stewards</b> determine data policies and set forth a plan to enforce compliance with those policies</li>
			<li><b>Data Quality</b> references the provenance (where did this data come from), completeness, accuracy, and fitness-to-purpose for a particular dataset</li>
			<li><b>Master Data Management</b> is a component of data governance that maintains the "official" reference copy of data to ensure consistent application across the organization</li>
		</ul>
	</div>

		<ul>

<section>
	<div class="full">
		<h2>Data Storytelling</h2>
		<h4>Storytelling = Visualization + Narrative + Context</h4>
		<p><i>“It’s the context around the data that provides value and that’s what will make people listen and engage.”</i> --James Richardson, Senior Director Analyst, Gartner</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Good narratives have a point of view and engage the audience across many different cognitive levels (not just analytical)</li>
			<li>Make it personal! (emotional engagement)</li>
			<li>Simplify with metaphor, anecdote, etc (trigger imagination)</li>
			<li>Build a narrative (ex: <a href="https://www.watershedlrs.com/blog/data-storytelling-heros-journey">Hero's journey</a>)</li>
		</ul>
	</div>
	<aside class="notes">
	Gartner has some good thinking, but Karen Dietz has a great counterpoint.
	</aside>
</section>

<section>
	<div class="full">
		<h2>Matching Reporting to Your Audience</h2>
	</div>
	<div class="half text-align-left">
		<ul>
			<li>What are my goals for this particular story? What outcome(s) am I shooting for?</li>
			<li>What have I learned from the data</li>
			<li>What does this mean to my organization? How does this relate to the specific audience/stakeholders I’m addressing.</li>
			<li>Do I have enough data to address the question?</li>
			<li>How can I express the data AND insights as simply as possible to incite action?</li>
		</ul>
	</div>
