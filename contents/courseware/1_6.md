<section data-background="#0747A6">
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2 class="color-white">Exercise 0: Answer the <br>"Big Three"</h2>
        <p class="color-white">For a recent data project, or for an existing data workflow, answer the "Big Three" post-mortem questions.</p>
        <ul>
            <li class="color-white">What worked that you'd absolutely do again without change?</li>
            <li class="color-white">What didn't work that you would never do again?</li>
            <li class="color-white">What worked, but you would do again differently? How?</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="half">
    	<h2 class="color-white">[IMAGE]</h2>
    </div>
    <aside class="notes">
    Making sure that everyone comes to the postmortem with their thoughts in order, and structured around similar questions is important to ensure that everyone is working from the same playbook.
    <br><br>
    There are many subjective metrics that you can gather from participants (in addition to the broader quantitative metrics of the project that can help frame the process), but it essentially boils down to three big questions:
    </aside>
</section>

    <h1>Qualitative & Quantitative</h1>
  </div>
  <aside class="notes">
  Notes
  </aside>
</section>

<section>
	<div class="half">
		<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>
	</div>
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2>Qualitative is important, but hard to measure</h2>
        <ul>
            <li>The "Big Three" questionnaire</li>
            <li>"Good" vs "Bad" outcomes</li>
            <li>"Satisfied" stakeholders</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <aside class="notes">
    Notes
    </aside>
</section>

<section>
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2>Quantitative is easier to measure, but hard to filter</h2>
        <ul>
            <li>Goals met?</li>
            <li>Numbers changed?</li>
            <li>Graphs well</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="half">
    	<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>
    </div>
    <aside class="notes">
    The pre-meeting questionnaire is great qualitative feedback, but making sure that you have measurable objectives of a data project is part of general best practices. 
    <br><br>
    Making sure that you have gathered the results of the project itself can be great validation (or contrast) for the qualitative objectives. 
    </aside>
</section>

<section>
	<div class="one-third">
		<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>
	</div>
    <div class="one-third text-align-left">
        <h2>By Your Powers Combined!</h2>
        <ul>
            <li>Combining feedback into a single summary can be hugely beneficial</li>
            <li>Try to give context with both Quantitative & Qualitative metrics</li>
            <li>Make sure everyone's voice is included to help spur discussion</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="one-third">
    	<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>
    </div>
    <aside class="notes">
    Writing a good summary before diving in can help provide clarity and direction for the postmortem. 
    <br><br>
    Combining the qualitative and quantitative can help to unearth some very interesting trends.
    <br><br>
    <ul>
    <li>Did the project meet all goals but the team felt it was inefficient?</li>>
    <li>Did the project fail all goals but the team was happy with their performance?</li>
    </ul>
    </aside>
</section>

		<p class="color-white">Based on your current process, what sorts of things do you evaluate to determine success?</p>
		<p class="color-white">What other things <b>should</b> you consider as it relates to data?</p>

	Write down your current topics when it comes to evaluating success.

	There is no right answer here, we're just trying to establish a baseline and discuss additional things that may help.
	</aside>
</section>

<section>
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2>Team Effectiveness</h2>
        <ul>
        	<li>Did you have the right people?</li>
        	<li>What methods of communication did you use?</li>
        	<li>Were handoffs and deadlines effectively communicated</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="half">
    	<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>
    </div>
    <aside class="notes">
    Notes
    </aside>
</section>

<section>
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2>Project Overall Success</h2>
        <ul>
        	<li>What success metrics did you establish before the project started</li>
        	<li>Did you meet all or most of those metrics</li>
        	<li>Why or why not?</li>
        	<li>What general qualitative sentiments did people have about the project?</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="half">
    	<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>
    </div>
    <aside class="notes">
    Notes
    </aside>
</section>

<section>
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2>Individual Project Goals</h2>
        <ul>
        	<li>Break down some of the individual goals of the project</li>
        	<li>How well did you meet these goals?</li>
        	<li>Were there more difficulties in one area than another?</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="half">
    	<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>
    </div>
    <aside class="notes">
    Notes
    </aside>
</section>

<section>
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2>Stretch Goals</h2>
        <ul>
        	<li>Were there any stretch goals established for the project?</li>
        	<li>Were you able meet these goals (or could you have)?</li>
        	<li>Were these truly stretch goals?</li>
        </ul>

        	<li>Processes</li>
        	<li>Models</li>
        	<li>Data Sources</li>

<section>
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2>Quality of Output</h2>
        <ul>
        	<li>How were insights delivered?</li>
        	<li>Who was the target audience?</li>
        	<li>Was it effective?</li>
        	<li>What could have been done to improve?</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="half">
    	<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>

<section>
    <div class="half text-align-left">
        <h2>Auditability</h2>
        <ul>
        	<li>Was the project documented end-to-end?</li>
        	<li>Could a new employee follow the process end-to-end without special instruction?</li>
        	<li>Could <b>you</b> follow the exact same steps and achieve the same results (repeatability)?</li>
        </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="half">
    	<h2>[IMAGE]</h2>

        <h2>Ethical Handling of Data</h2>
        <ul>
        	<li>What ethical standards do you apply to data work (if any)?</li>
        	<li>What regulations are appropriate for your locality/industry?</li>
        	<li>How well did you meet expectations?</li>
        	<li>Are there ethical considerations that you should apply even if not mandated?</li>
